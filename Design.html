        DESIGN

        Inspiration

            With the increasing incidence and mortality of cancer, there are many limitations of the current anti-tumor treatments. So the original idea of our project was to improve the cancer therapies, we were looking for a new treatment that combines the strengths of the current approaches. Later, we found that oncolytic virus therapy has recently been recognized as a promising new therapeutic approach for cancer treatment. It combines immune therapy, gene therapy and advantages of targeted drugs, which can selectively replicate in and kill cancer cells without harming the normal tissues. In contrast to gene therapy where a virus is used as a mere carrier for transgene delivery, oncolytic virus therapy uses the virus itself as an active drug reagent.

            Although the advantages of oncolytic virus are obvious, the ability of the virus to spread in tumor also limit the efficacy of oncolytic virus. To expand the anti-tumor effect of M1, we further studied the mechanism of M1, and then we have hypothesized that if M1 is combined with a chemotherapy drug that acts on DNA double-stranded, the combined treatment could induce better antitumor effect efficiently. Therefore, Actinomycin D, Etoposide and other drugs were selected, and it was found that actinomycin D had the most significant effect. Furthermore, it even has a powerful effect on enhancing M1's infection to tumor cells.

            However, Actinomycin D has many side effects, such as myelosuppression, gastrointestinal reactions, liver function impairment etc. Accordingly, it is of vital importance to improve its targeting ability, which is exactly one of the most significant advantages of M1.

            Previous studies have reported that the prodrug of actinomycin D can be activated by nitroreductase. Therefore, we designed this system to combine M1 virus with actinomycin D for cancer treatment. We engineered M1 armed with nitroreductase, and after nitroreductase was delivered to the cancer cell by M1 and expressed, the enzyme can activate subsequently administered prodrugs of actinomycin D to potent drugs. Further, actinomycin D can promote the replication of M1 virus, which made up a constant reinforcing system. In addition, in order to ensure the controllability and safety of the system, we used engineered riboswitches to control gene expression of M1 armed with nitroreductase.

Finally, we proved that this project was feasible.

        Directed evolution

        Since the interaction between virus and host is extremely complex and diverse, and a large number of molecular interaction regulatory networks have not been clarified, the ability to use these molecular mechanisms for virus modification is limited. The biggest difference between oncolytic virus and replicator-deficient virus vectors and other anticancer drugs is that OVs can replicate and mutate, and thus has the potential to evolve under the pressure of natural selection, and the direction of evolution can be directed in a controlled laboratory environment.

        Therefore, we considered to construct the M1 with low fidelity RNA polymerase to increase the probability of mutation caused by adaptation. In addition, we inserted ligands of receptors that are highly expressed in some tumors, which aims to enhance the affinity between M1 and insensitive tumor cells.

        Pros:

        1. Increase the fitness and oncoselectivity of previously engineered oncolytic virus

        2. Overcome some of the limitations imposed by the complexity of virus–host interactions.

        3. Combine all the mutation site, it could obtain the high efficiency of new broad-spectrum oncolytic virus M1.

        Cons:

        1. The mutation is uncertain. It is hard to predict whether M1 will evolve into a new virus which has a boarder anti-tumor spectrum and stronger anti-tumor effect.

        3D culture

        Three-dimensional cell culture model can better simulate the natural developing environment in vivo. 3D cell culture can establish physiological cell-cell and cell-extracellular matrix interaction and restore the metabolic pattern of cells in tissues, so as to be closer to the characteristics of natural tissues. Its physiological correlation is significantly stronger than traditional two-dimensional cell culture. This is important in cancer research. Recent studies have reported that a variety of oncolytic viruses have developed a new generation of oncolytic viruses using three-dimensional culture models, such as adenovirus for the treatment of colon cancer, reovirus for the treatment of glioblastoma, herpes simplex virus for the treatment of melanoma and breast cancer, etc.

        We considered to use 3D culture to improve the fitness of M1 to insensitive cells, in order to expand the anti-tumor spectrum of M1 and enhance its anti- tumor effect.

        Pros:

        1. It could not only reduce the fitness of normal cells and improve the safety of M1, but also enhance the oncolysis of M1 and develop the next generation of effective oncolytic virus M1.

        2. The data that we obtained from 3D culture are more clinically relevant.

        Cons:

        One major problem in the field is the lack of a standard approach in 3D cultures, whereas 2D cell culture practice is standard and roughly comparable.
