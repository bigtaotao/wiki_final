        Basic parts

        Nitroreductase

        Nitroreductase is an enzyme that can catalyze the reduction of nitro aromatic compounds to aromatic amines. In our project, Nitroreductase was linked into the genome of M1 virus to give M1 virus with the function of converting prodrug into Actinomycin D, which has the ability of contributing to the proliferation of M1 virus and assisting M1 virus to kill cancer cells.

        Type: Protein coding sequences

        CMV promoter

        CMV promoter is recognized as the most powerful promoter to start the gene expression of eukaryon. In our project, we use the CMV promoter to help us construct the ISA fragment[1].

        Type:Promoter

        EGFR

        A series of ligands were integrated into the genome of M1 virus, EGFR is one of them. Such a part overexpressed in our engineered M1 virus could enhance the affinity between M1 virus and cancer cells so that it could improve the possibility of favorable mutations.

        Type: Protein coding sequences

        Her2-recoptor:

        Her2R is also one of the integrated ligands. It could improve the affinity between M1 virus and cancer cells such as MCF-7 & BT474 to increase the possibility of favorable mutations.

        Type: Protein coding sequences

        EGFRvⅢ:

        This is ligand is a variant type of EGFR, which means it plays the same role in our project as EGFR.

        Type: Protein coding sequences

        SV40 polyA:

        SV40 PolyA is a specific sequence from Simian virus 40, which could shut down the process of gene transcription[1].

        Type: polyA tail

        eGFP:

        eGFP is a type of GFP derivatives by mutation. This mutation greatly improved the spectral characteristics of GFP, resulting in increased fluorescence, photostability, and a shift of the major excitation peak to 488 nm, with the peak emission kept at 509 nm. This matched the spectral characteristics of commonly available FITC filter sets, so it could be used by the researcher more efficiently, such as using it in mammalian cells.

        Type: Protein coding sequences

        Composite parts

        Riboswitches

        Riboswitches is an element comprising a ribozyme actuator and RNA aptamer sensor, which could bind to a series of small molecular metabolites and iron ions to regulates transcription, translation, splicing and the stability of RNA.[3]

        In our project, we use a kind of riboswitch developed from the hammerhead ribozyme from the satellite RNA of tobacco ringspot virus (sTRSV) conjugated to a theophylline aptamer. It’s an OFF-type riboswitch, which inhibits ribozyme cleavage in the absence of theophylline, but addition of theophylline allows the ribozyme to form its active conformation and initiate cleavage, turning gene expression off. By adding theophylline or not, we can control the expression of M1 virus’s gene, which greatly improves the safety of M1 virus and efficiency of gene expression.

        Type: composite parts
