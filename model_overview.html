        Abstract

            Modeling is a way to help reduce time and cost of work, especially in the experiment. The cost in exploring the suitable condition for ex- periment is high, including the reagents purchase, lab cost, and probable consulting cost. And experiments often begin on experience, including the collection time. After the method is established, can we have a prediction on its effect? Because of these reasons, our modeling is necessary.

            Our modeling contains 4 parts:M1 Cultivation, Injection System, Saty and Effectiveness and Future. Each section relate with the spe- cific period of our experiment. And the target of each section is to solve the problem that happened in the experiment and probably in the future.

            In the section of expanding the gene library of M1, we compared many structures and parameters of models and finally selected our differ- ential equations. We improved the mutation model to describe the change of number of M1 and the situation of RNA sequence mutation accurate- ly. And this method was also used in the next section. As a result, we calculated 72h as the best time for collection, which could be confirmed by experiment. We provided the method which can be used in the next section. Firstly, it was considered that there were uninfected vero cells, infected vero cells and M1 in the culture medium, and the change of the number of the three microorganisms was related to the number at the current moment. Based on the biological characteristics of viruses grow- ing in culture medium and the growth rules of other types of viruses in culture medium, functional differential equations were established to de- scribe the number of the three kinds of microorganisms over time. For the differential equations in the model, we adopted Runge-Kutta algorithm. The numerical solution of the equations was obtained with the help of MATLAB, which reflected the relation between the number of M1 and it's growing time.

            Then, we analyzed the process of the directed evolution of M1. Dur- ing the growth of M1, we hoped to accumulate useful mutations by con- trolling its growth environment, so as to enhance its effect on tumors and even expand its range of action.

            During the process, each site of the RNA was assumed to mutate independently. That is, the mutational probability of each site was not affected by the mutation of other sites. And we also assumed that within a certain time, the number of mutated sites of M1 obeyed Poisson Process. This enabled us to know the change rule of mutation sites number after the M1 mutation rate reached 0.1%, which provided the best time for the experiment to collect the target M1 virus.

            In the section of Animal Experiment, we established the models ac- cording to M1’s and actinomycin D’s concentration development in the area of tumor and the whole body of mouse.

            In the area of tumor, we established the ER-M1 model which con- sidered the enzymatic reaction was caused by M1 injection and enzyme production. This model was tranformed according to the Dynamic Sys- tem of enzymatic reaction. The rate of the reaction was adaptive to the time-varying titer of M1 and the concentration of enzyme, and then we could realize the development of the complex time-varying concentration of actinomycin D. Furthermore, we considered the injection of M1 and the damage of actinomycin D to tumor cell. By the improved differential equations model above and innovative Mixed Michaelis-Menten Equation, we simulated the movement of M1 and drug therapy more accurately.

            For the whole body of mouse, M1 targeting ability and prodrug con- centration variation in the local and global relationship were taken into consideration. And we used compartment model to obtain the relation- ship between medicine concentration in blood and in targeting area under the rapid intravenous injection. Then we could acquire the initial titer of M1 and prodrug concentration in the tumor area. In addition, we provid- ed the method of estimating the distribution of drug in the specific area to predict the upper and lower bound of concentration. On the basis of it, we could control the side effect and maintain the curative level of drug. From the macroscopic injection to microcosmic variation of medicine con- centration, virus titer and the tumor cell concentration, the mixed model depicted the process elaborately. Thus, we got that around 72h is the best injection time interval and about 3.455µg/mL as the best injection volume. The model got sufficient theoretical support and was innovative. It provided vital reference value for the establishment of treatment regime.

            It is indubitable that doctors wish to apply the successful excellent medical method on the body of patients in the future to solve the problem that could not be solved by traditional methods. To evaluate the probable curative effect on human beings, we collected large amounts of medical data of mouse and human, and also read a lot of references. Then we compared a great many regression methods(polynomial, linear, etc) and classifiers (including SVM, RF, AdaBoost etc.) by using the AUC and accuracy rate to get the Ensemble Classifier with the best configuration. We luckily found that M1 method could make the tumor decrease about 60% in 48h in prediction based on data. However, we could not just apply the treatment strategies of mouse to man directly due to the complexity of human body. What’s more, the traditional treatment regime that could not be strictly time-varying could not always have good effect. In fact, oncotherapy is more likely to be the game between tumor and doctor, so we added the DTR model on the basis of reinforcement learning to provide the dynamic treatment regime for the future.

            In addition, we adopted the Path Model that SYSU-Medicine used in 2016, and made the change of the picture more smooth by using neutral network, by which the medicine distribution change can be visualized in the body of mouse with time. And we collected the data from the experiments, references and databases to guarantee the accuracy of our models. But some data was difficult to collect or the measurement of them had big error. In that case, we simulated the process based on our model. And in the actual process, contact ratio has its time-varying distribution. We used Gaussian Mixture Model to fit the distribution, and we could got the estimated value of contact ratio based on the position. In the end, we tried to package the models and algorithms to make them simple in practical use, especially for synthetic biology researchers and medicine science researchers. In the future, we will consider to reduce the algorithm complexity and add the improved Cellular Automation to adjust our model for higher accuracy and better efficiency. In a whole, our modeling result has the advantages of elaboration, accuracy, innovation, intelligence and convenience, giving our experiment important support and vital evaluation. It pushes our experimental result into models and data in order to make the research can stand on the shoulder of us and make it better.
